# Oceaniaya Revenue Sprint — 7 Steps

## Purpose

Move Oceaniaya from prepared assets to the first serious commercial event: paid audit, partner call, investor memo review, invoice request, or customer advance.

## Guardrail

All external communication is company-first. Messages focus on market, offer, workflow value, audit scope, and next milestone. No founder private circumstances are included.

## Step 1 — Use the safest commercial wedge

Primary offer:

AI Compliance & Automation Audit — EUR/CHF 7,500

Why:

It is easier to buy than a full pilot and creates a professional path into EUR/CHF 20,000–35,000 implementation work.

## Step 2 — Prioritize three buyer paths

1. Netherlands premium hospitality and luxury service operators
2. Swiss regulated documentation and knowledge teams
3. Strategic partners that can co-sell audits into existing clients

## Step 3 — Use private professional routes only

Allowed routes:

- warm introductions
- official company inquiry forms
- professional networks
- angel networks
- partner/business development channels
- procurement or innovation channels

Avoid:

- random personal accounts
- private family contacts
- public emotional posts
- identity-based targeting
- claims of guaranteed returns

## Step 4 — Message sequence

Message 1:

Ask whether the correct team would review a one-page overview.

Message 2:

If interested, send the relevant one-pager and offer a short audit scoping call.

Message 3:

If qualified, send the audit scope and invoice/payment route.

## Step 5 — Qualification rules

A target is qualified only if at least one is true:

- has premium or regulated workflows
- has internal documentation or service bottlenecks
- already buys consulting, AI, digital transformation, hospitality tech, or enterprise software
- has a clear buyer persona such as COO, CIO, Head of Operations, Guest Experience Lead, QA Lead, Regulatory Lead, or Innovation Lead

## Step 6 — Payment readiness

Before accepting paid work, prepare:

- invoice/payment route
- delivery scope
- refund/cancellation terms
- data and privacy boundary
- no-production-risk disclaimer
- calendar route for audit scoping

## Step 7 — First-money success condition

The sprint succeeds if one of these happens:

- one EUR/CHF 7,500 audit is sold
- one EUR 10,000–12,000 concierge sprint is requested
- one EUR/CHF 20,000–35,000 pilot is requested
- one strategic co-sell partner asks for a call
- one angel/operator asks for the memo

## Immediate next action

Create a short audit scope document that can be sent after a prospect says yes.
