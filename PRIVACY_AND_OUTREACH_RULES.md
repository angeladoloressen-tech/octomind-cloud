# Privacy and Outreach Rules

## Purpose

Protect the founder's private identity and keep all public, institutional, sales, and investor messaging focused on the business case.

## Default rule

Do not disclose sensitive private identity details in:

- public repositories
- public documents
- investor memos
- institutional emails
- sales emails
- prospecting sheets
- pitch materials
- proposals

## Allowed public framing

Use this framing by default:

- founder from a large, low-income family
- 45 years old
- educated and technically ambitious
- building through financial pressure
- seeking financial freedom through serious experimentation
- building Octomind as an Autonomous Company OS
- looking for strategic feedback, introductions, paid pilots, or serious believer capital

## Disallowed public framing

Do not use:

- private sexual identity as a funding hook
- private identity as pity
- private identity as decoration
- incorrect gender assumptions
- charity-style language
- desperation language
- guaranteed revenue claims
- guaranteed unicorn claims

## Private identity rule

Private identity may only be used in a message when the founder explicitly approves it for that specific recipient and that specific message.

## Money-language rule

The core financial story is not identity-based.

The story is:

A technically ambitious founder with no meaningful financial runway has built a proof stack and now needs first revenue, pilot capital, or one serious believer to turn Octomind into a revenue-generating company.

## First Euro rule

Every revenue action should move toward one of these:

- 490 EUR diagnostic
- 1,500 EUR build sprint
- 10,000 EUR command center installation
- 25,000 EUR plus AI Operations Reset
- 25,000 to 100,000 EUR believer capital or pilot capital

## Safety boundary

No bulk sending. No private data scraping. No payment execution. No identity disclosure without explicit approval.
