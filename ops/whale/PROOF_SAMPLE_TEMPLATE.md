# Whale Proof Sample Template

Date: 2026-05-25
System: Whale Revenue Desk

## Purpose

Give a whale prospect a small useful proof before asking for money.

This is the customer-foot-in-the-door step.
It should feel helpful, specific, and serious.
It must not expose internal system secrets.

## Structure

### 1. Company
Name:
Segment:
Website or public page:

### 2. Public signal path inspected
Example paths:
- demo request page
- partner page
- contact page
- event inquiry form
- affiliate/reseller page
- booking page
- product page

### 3. One observed friction point
Keep it concrete.
Do not invent private data.

Example:
The partner page asks for contact but does not clarify what happens after submission.

### 4. Why it matters
Connect to business risk.

Example:
When the next step is unclear, qualified partners may hesitate or send incomplete information.

### 5. One practical improvement
Small and obvious.

Example:
Add a short line explaining the review timeline, required information, and what kind of partner is a strong fit.

### 6. Paid next step
Soft, not aggressive.

Example:
If useful, I can map the full inquiry-to-follow-up route in an Executive Signal Audit.

## Sample proof note

Subject: Small signal-path note for [Company]

Hi [Name/Team],

I took a quick look at your public [demo/partner/booking/contact] path and noticed one small friction point:

[Observed friction point]

Why it matters:
[Business risk]

One simple improvement:
[Practical improvement]

This is just a small proof sample. If useful, I can map the full signal path and first recovery actions in a scoped Executive Signal Audit.

Best,
[Sender]

## Rules

- Keep it under 180 words.
- Mention one friction point only.
- Do not attach a giant report in the first touch.
- Do not claim access to private analytics.
- Do not promise revenue increase.
- Do not sound desperate.
- Do not sell before showing the proof.
